01 / Overview

What Cinder solves

Buying native XRP across chains usually forces a buyer to trust a broker, expose their intended price, or release payment before delivery is proven.

Cinder replaces that workflow with an on-chain RFQ. A buyer locks a Flare token and specifies an XRPL destination. Makers submit hashes of their prices during bidding, then reveal after the deadline. The lowest valid quote wins and escrow releases only after the XRP payment is verified.

Flare is required, not decorative.

Coston2 enforces escrow and deadlines, FTSOv2 constrains execution to a live XRP/USD price band, and FDC proves the corresponding XRPL payment before funds can move.

02 / Architecture

One trade, four verifiable stages

01 / LOCKFund escrow

The buyer locks dcUSD with an amount, destination commitment and deadlines.

02 / MATCHCommit and reveal

Makers hide prices with salted hashes, then reveal inside the allowed window.

03 / DELIVERSend native XRP

The winner pays the XRPL destination using the unique payment reference.

04 / VERIFYRelease escrow

FDC validates the XRPL payment before the contract pays the maker.

Price protection

At finalization, the winning quote must remain inside the contract's permitted deviation from the FTSOv2 XRP/USD feed. A stale or manipulated quote cannot finalize simply because it won the auction.

03 / Testnet guide

Run a real Coston2 RFQ

The Testnet Lab submits real transactions. Use a test-only wallet and never send mainnet assets.

  1. Connect to Coston2. Open the Lab and connect a wallet on chain ID 114.
  2. Get gas. Use the linked Flare faucet to obtain C2FLR.
  3. Claim demo liquidity. Claim 100 dcUSD once for that wallet.
  4. Create the RFQ. Approve and lock 10 dcUSD. The on-chain bidding countdown begins.
  5. Commit during bidding. Submit the suggested FTSO-valid quote before the countdown reaches zero.
  6. Reveal during the reveal phase. The button unlocks automatically after bidding closes.
  7. Finalize after reveal closes. Open the resulting transaction hash in the Coston2 explorer.

Important: Do not change wallets, browsers, or clear site storage between Commit and Reveal. The locally generated salt is required.

06 / Trust model

What stays private

During bidding, Cinder publishes only keccak256(abi.encode(rfqId, maker, price, salt)). Makers cannot read a submitted price and undercut it before bidding closes. A reveal that does not match its commitment is rejected.

Prices become public during reveal. Cinder provides trustless bidding privacy, not permanent TEE confidentiality. Matching requires no Cinder operator key, and settlement requires an FDC-verified XRPL payment.
